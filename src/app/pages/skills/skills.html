“On Learning Slowly”

I used to rush tutorials.
Skim docs. Copy code. Move fast.
But I never remembered what I stole.
Now I read the docs like novels — slowly, with curiosity.
Learning isn’t speedrunning. It’s gardening.

“Coffee, Keyboards, and the Weight of Thought”

My desk is quiet but full:
Cold brew. Crumbs. A terminal window half-closed.
I sit here and build things that don’t exist yet.
Isn’t that wild?
Just me, a keyboard, and belief.

“AI Feels Like Talking to Myself with Better Grammar”

It completes my sentences.
Suggests better ones.
And yet, I wonder if I’m becoming more concise… or just less human.

“What the Commit Message Didn’t Say”

fix minor issue with nav
But that issue wasn’t minor to me.
It represented the tenth thing I got wrong that week.
Some days I wish version control applied to life.

“A Bug That Broke Me (Then Built Me Again)”

It was two lines.
Two lines in a tangled forest.
I lost a week chasing ghosts through console logs and caffeine.
But when I found it, I didn’t feel relief — I felt humbled.
The system wasn’t wrong. My assumptions were.

“The Version of Me That Never Learned to Let Go”

I hold onto ideas too long.
Projects, people, lines of code.
Maybe because letting go feels like disrespecting the effort.
But keeping things past their time clutters the mind.
Even in memory, you need garbage collection.
